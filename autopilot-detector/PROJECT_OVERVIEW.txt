────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT IS AUTOPILOT DETECTOR?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Autopilot Detector is a browser tool that watches how you use the internet
and tells you when your brain has switched to "autopilot mode" — that mindless,
zombie-like state where you scroll without thinking, hop between tabs aimlessly,
or watch video after video without really choosing to.

The goal is simple: help you become more intentional about how you spend your
time online. Instead of looking at your phone and realising an hour just
vanished, Autopilot Detector catches the drift WHILE it's happening and
nudges you back.

Think of it as a fitness tracker, but for your attention span.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW THE APPLICATION WORKS — STEP BY STEP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1 — You declare your intent
  Before you start browsing, you open the extension and tell it WHY you're
  going online. Options are:
    · STUDY        — you're researching or learning something
    · TUTORIAL     — you're following a guide or walkthrough
    · ENTERTAINMENT — you're watching something deliberately
    · PRODUCTIVITY  — you're working on something
    · PASSIVE       — you're just chilling, no goal

  This is important because the app judges your behaviour AGAINST your
  stated intention. Watching a 2-hour movie when you said "ENTERTAINMENT"
  is fine. Watching a 2-hour movie when you said "STUDY" is autopilot.

STEP 2 — The extension silently watches
  The Chrome extension runs quietly in the background and collects signals
  every few seconds about HOW you're browsing:
    · How fast you're scrolling
    · How often you switch between tabs
    · How much time you spend active vs passively sitting on a page
    · How deep you've scrolled into a page (doomscroll detection)
    · How many times an infinite feed (like YouTube/Twitter) has reloaded

STEP 3 — The data is sent to the brain (the server)
  Every 4 seconds, those signals are packaged up and sent in real-time
  to the backend server over a live connection (WebSocket). The server
  is like the "brain" of the application — it does all the heavy thinking.

STEP 4 — Your Autopilot Score is calculated
  Every ~24 seconds (6 batches of data), the server runs the numbers and
  produces an "Autopilot Score" from 0–100:
    · 0–40   = Highly Focused (you're doing great)
    · 41–74  = Drifting Slightly (getting a bit mindless)
    · 75–100 = Autopilot Detected (intervention needed)

  The score is made up of 4 sub-scores:
    · Focus Fragmentation  — how often you jump between different things
    · Passive Ratio        — how long you've been a passive zombie
    · Cognitive Drift      — how far your behaviour has strayed from your intent
    · Doomscroll Probability — how much you're trapped in an infinite scroll loop

STEP 5 — The AI classifies what you're looking at
  On top of the behaviour signals, the server uses an AI (Groq's language
  model) to read the title of the page you're on and judge whether it
  actually matches your stated intent. Watching a cat video when you said
  "STUDY" will push your score up faster.

STEP 6 — Interventions are triggered
  When your score crosses certain thresholds, the app steps in:
    · Score > 50  → NUDGE    : a small banner appears ("Hey, you're drifting...")
    · Score > 65  → PAUSE    : a stronger overlay appears, asks you to pause
    · Score > 80  → REFLECTION: asks you to reflect on what you wanted to do
    · Score > 90  → SLEEP MODE: the most serious intervention

  These interventions are generated by AI (Groq) and are personalised based
  on your session history — it references past sessions where you felt
  good vs drained to make the message more relevant.

STEP 7 — You see it all on the dashboard
  The web dashboard (localhost:3000) shows you:
    · Your live score updating in real time
    · A timeline chart of how your score changed during the session
    · An analytics view with a heatmap of your worst autopilot hours
    · A mood vs. drift chart showing how browsing affects your wellbeing
    · A session history log

STEP 8 — After the session, you rate your mood
  When you end a session, the extension asks "How do you feel?" on a scale
  of 1–5 (Drained to Energized). This data is saved and plotted against
  your autopilot scores so you can see the emotional cost of mindless
  browsing over time.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 2 FEATURES — WHAT'S NEW (explained simply)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. POMODORO FOCUS TIMER
   ─────────────────────
   You can now start a built-in 25-minute focus countdown directly from
   the extension popup. After 25 minutes, it notifies you to take a
   5-minute break. During the break, all interventions are paused —
   the app knows you're resting on purpose. Then it resets and you
   can start the next focus block.

   Why it matters: Pomodoro is a proven productivity technique. Pairing
   it with autopilot detection means you get both structure AND awareness.

2. DAILY SITE BUDGETS
   ───────────────────
   You can now set a daily time limit per website. For example:
   "I only want 30 minutes of YouTube per day."

   When you've used up your budget, the extension puts up a full-screen
   wall on that site. You can still override it if you really need to,
   but you have to consciously choose to do so. Budgets reset at midnight.

   Why it matters: Awareness alone isn't enough. Hard limits create
   friction that breaks habitual behaviour.

3. MOOD TRACKING
   ──────────────
   After every session, the extension pops up a simple "How do you feel?"
   prompt — rated from Drained 😞 to Energized 😊.

   Over time, the dashboard plots this against your autopilot scores in
   a correlation chart. You can literally see: "Every time my score
   spikes above 70, I end up feeling drained." That's the kind of
   personal data that actually changes behaviour.

4. INFINITE SCROLL (DOOMSCROLL) DETECTION
   ─────────────────────────────────────────
   The extension now specifically detects infinite scroll traps —
   like YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Twitter/X, Instagram Reels.

   It tracks:
   · How far down the page you've scrolled
   · How many times the feed has automatically refreshed with new content

   The more you're stuck in an infinite scroll loop, the more it pushes
   up your "Doomscroll Probability" sub-score.

5. PASSIVE MODE
   ─────────────
   A new intent option that lets you say "I'm just chilling today, I'm
   not trying to be productive."

   In Passive mode, the app still watches your behaviour but it only
   gives you very light, gentle nudges — it won't throw a big
   intervention at you for watching YouTube when you've already said
   that's what you're doing.

TECH STACK — WHAT TECHNOLOGY IS USED AND WHY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CHROME EXTENSION (what lives in your browser)
  Built with : TypeScript + Vite
  Why        : TypeScript is JavaScript with safety checks — it catches
               mistakes before the code runs. Vite bundles all the code
               into something Chrome can actually load.
  What it does:
    · Watches your browsing behaviour every 2 seconds
    · Manages the popup UI (intent picker, dashboard, budgets tab)
    · Controls Pomodoro timer via Chrome's built-in alarm system
    · Shows overlays directly on web pages (mood check, budget wall,
      interventions)
    · Sends data to the server over a live socket connection

──────────────────────────────────────────────

BACKEND API (the brain — runs on your computer or a server)
  Built with : NestJS (a TypeScript framework for Node.js)
  Why        : NestJS gives a structured, organized way to build the
               server — like having clearly labelled departments in a
               company rather than one messy room.
  What it does:
    · Receives behavioural signals from the extension in real time
    · Calculates the autopilot score every ~24 seconds
    · Stores sessions, scores, and mood entries in the database
    · Triggers AI-generated interventions when score thresholds are crossed
    · Serves data to the web dashboard

──────────────────────────────────────────────

WEBSOCKET (the live connection)
  Built with : Socket.IO
  Why        : Normal web requests are like sending a letter — you ask,
               you wait, you get a reply. WebSockets are like a phone
               call that stays open. The extension and server can talk
               to each other instantly and continuously.
  What it does:
    · Streams behavioural signals from the extension to the server
    · Pushes updated scores from the server back to the extension AND
      the web dashboard instantly — no refresh needed

──────────────────────────────────────────────

DATABASE (long-term memory)
  Built with : PostgreSQL + Prisma ORM + pgvector extension
  Why        : PostgreSQL is a powerful, reliable database. Prisma is
               a tool that lets developers talk to the database using
               normal code instead of raw database commands. pgvector
               adds support for storing AI "memory embeddings."
  What it stores:
    · User accounts
    · Every browsing session (start time, intent, page category)
    · Autopilot scores over time
    · Interventions that were shown
    · Mood ratings
    · AI "embeddings" — mathematical fingerprints of each session
      used for smarter future recommendations

──────────────────────────────────────────────

CACHE / MESSAGE QUEUE (the short-term memory and job manager)
  Built with : Redis + BullMQ
  Why        : Redis is an ultra-fast memory store — like a whiteboard
               the server can read/write in milliseconds. BullMQ uses
               Redis to manage background jobs (tasks the server needs
               to do without making you wait).
  What it does:
    · Buffers the last 100 signals per session for score calculation
    · Enforces intervention cooldowns (so you're not spammed every 30s)
    · Queues and retries AI embedding generation jobs after sessions end
    · Tracks daily site usage for budget enforcement

──────────────────────────────────────────────

AI / LANGUAGE MODELS (the smart layer)
  Built with : Groq API (fast inference) + Google Gemini API (embeddings)
  Why        : Groq runs AI models at extremely high speed — much faster
               than OpenAI. Gemini is used for generating vector
               embeddings (mathematical representations of text).
  What they do:
    · Groq classifies whether a page title matches your stated intent
      (e.g. "cat compilation #47" when intent is STUDY → mismatch)
    · Groq generates personalised intervention messages referencing
      your past sessions
    · Gemini turns session descriptions into vectors stored in the DB,
      enabling the AI to find "sessions similar to this one" for
      smarter nudges

──────────────────────────────────────────────

WEB DASHBOARD (the analytics interface)
  Built with : Next.js 16 (React framework) + Recharts + Tailwind CSS
  Why        : Next.js handles both the server-rendered pages and the
               client-side interactivity. Recharts draws the graphs.
  What it shows:
    · Live score widget (updates in real time via WebSocket)
    · Drift timeline chart (score over time during a session)
    · Weekly heatmap (which hours/days are your worst autopilot periods)
    · Mood × Drift correlation chart
    · Session history
    · AI Coach chat interface

──────────────────────────────────────────────

MONOREPO STRUCTURE (how the code is organized)
  Built with : Turborepo + npm workspaces
  Why        : Instead of having three completely separate projects
               (extension, API, web), everything lives in one repository
               and shares common code. Like one filing cabinet with
               clearly labelled drawers instead of three separate ones.
  Packages:
    · apps/extension  — the Chrome extension
    · apps/api        — the NestJS backend
    · apps/web        — the Next.js dashboard
    · packages/shared — types and interfaces shared by all three
